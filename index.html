STEP 1

💧 Ghusl (Step-by-Step Ritual Washing Guide)

Ghusl is the obligatory full-body ritual bath given to a deceased Muslim. It must be performed with utmost gentleness, respect, and privacy. (Male Rules: Performed by trustworthy men)

  1. Privacy & Awrah: Place a cloth over the body from the navel to the knees. The private areas (Awrah) must remain covered at all times during washing.
  2. Cleaning Private Areas: The washer wears gloves and cleans the deceased's private areas gently with water without looking directly at the Awrah.
  3. Perform Wudu (Ablution): Perform standard Wudu for the deceased (wipe teeth and nostrils gently with wet cotton; do not pour water into nose or mouth; wash face, arms, wipe head, and wash feet).
  4. Washing the Body (3, 5, or 7 times): Wash with warm water mixed with Sidr (lote leaves) or mild soap. Start with the right side of the head and neck, then upper right body, upper left body, lower right body, and lower left body. Turn body onto left side to wash the back right, then onto right side to wash the back left.
  5. Final Rinse: The final rinse must contain a small amount of Camphor or non-alcoholic scent. Dry the body gently with a clean towel.
STEP 2

📜 Kafan (Step-by-Step Shrouding Guide)

Shrouding involves wrapping the clean, dried body in simple, unstitched white cotton sheets.

  1. Requirement for Men: 3 plain unstitched white sheets layered over each other. (For women, 5 garments: inner shirt/lifafah, head veil, waist wrap, and 2 outer sheets).
  2. Applying Fragrance (Attar): Apply non-alcoholic Attar/perfume to the prostration points of the deceased (forehead, nose, hands, knees, and feet).
  3. Wrapping: Fold the left side of the top sheet over the body, then the right side over the left. Repeat for all layers.
  4. Securing: Tie strips of cloth above the head, around the waist, and below the feet. (These strips are untied inside the grave before burial).
STEP 3

🤲 Swalatul Janazah (Funeral Prayer Guide)

A communal obligation (Fard Kifayah) performed entirely while standing. There is NO Ruku (bowing) and NO Sujood (prostration).

  1. 1st Takbeer ("Allahu Akbar"): Raise hands, place them on the chest, and silently recite Surah Al-Fatiha.
  2. 2nd Takbeer ("Allahu Akbar"): Say Takbeer and recite Durood-e-Ibrahim (the blessing upon Prophet Muhammad ﷺ as recited in regular prayer).
  3. 3rd Takbeer ("Allahu Akbar"): Say Takbeer and make sincere Dua for the deceased asking Allah for mercy, forgiveness, and Paradise.
  4. 4th Takbeer ("Allahu Akbar"): Say Takbeer, pause briefly, then perform Tasleem to the right ("Assalamu Alaikum wa Rahmatullah") to conclude the prayer.
STEP 4

🪦 Burial Practices (Step-by-Step Guide)

Lowering the deceased into the grave with dignity and honor.

  1. Lowering into Grave: Male relatives lower the body into the grave feet-first or from the side while saying: "Bismillahi wa 'ala millati Rasoolillah".
  2. Qibla Positioning: Place the body on its right side facing towards Makkah (Qibla). Untie the cloth strips at the head and feet.
  3. Closing Grave & Supplication: Cover the recess with soil or planks. Attendees pour 3 handfuls of soil into the grave. After filling, stand at the grave and pray for the deceased's steadfastness and forgiveness.
HATUA YA 1

💧 Ghusl (Hatua kwa Hatua za Osho la Maiti)

Osho la maiti hufanywa kwa heshima kubwa. Sehemu za siri (Uchi) zibaki zimefunikwa wakati wote wa kuosha.

  1. Kufunika uchi wa marehemu kuanzia kitovu hadi magotini.
  2. Kutawaza (Wudhu) marehemu bila kuingiza maji puani au mdomoni.
  3. Kuosha mwili mara 3, 5, au 7 kwa maji ya vuguvugu na Sidr (majani ya mkunazi) na Kafuri mwishoni.
HATUA YA 2

📜 Kafan (Hatua za Kuvika Sanda)

Kumfunika marehemu kwa vitambaa vyeupe visivyoshonwa. Wanaume sanda 3, Wanawake sanda 5.
